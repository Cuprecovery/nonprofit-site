How We Help

At CUP-RECOVERY, we support individuals in recovery by meeting essential, practical needs that help create stability during early and ongoing sober living.

  • Temporary housing assistance and sober living support
  • Food, groceries, and daily meal support
  • Hygiene essentials and basic personal care items
  • Limited transportation for work, meetings, and appointments through volunters,as available
  • Connection to faith-based and community recovery resources

These supports are offered as an expression of our faith and our commitment to serve others with compassion, accountability, and hope.

Matthew 25:31–46 (The Least Of These)

In this passage, Jesus teaches that when the Son of Man returns in glory, He will separate the righteous from the unrighteous based on how they responded to human need. Feeding the hungry, welcoming the stranger, clothing the naked, caring for the sick, and visiting those in prison are revealed not as optional acts of kindness, but as evidence of a transformed heart.

This teaching affirms that compassion, service, and obedience are central to the Christian life. What is done for the most vulnerable is counted by Christ as service done directly to Him.

Our Mission

Our mission is to walk alongside individuals facing addiction, homelessness, and life-controlling challenges by offering compassionate support and helping connect them to trusted recovery, housing, food, and community resources. We provide a safe, judgment-free environment rooted in faith, hope, and dignity, meeting people where they are without pressure, timelines, or program requirements.

Our Vision

Our vision is a future where no one seeking help feels alone or without options. As our organization grows, we aim to expand partnerships, increase access to life-changing resources, and pursue nonprofit 501(c)(3) status to strengthen our ability to serve the community with transparency, accountability, and care.
